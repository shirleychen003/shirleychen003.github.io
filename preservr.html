1. Overview

While past generations may have used physical photo albums and diaries to leave their legacies, our digital-native generation uses platforms like Instagram, TikTok, and Facebook to illustrate and tell their personal stories. Users lack reliable ways to safeguard their digital identities and narratives in the face of platform obsolescence, privacy risks, and shifting technological landscapes.

Preservr explored how an integrated tool suite can empower individuals to preserve their digital footprints while addressing challenges of organization, accessibility, sustainability, and heritage. The integrated tool suite allows people to curate their digital footprints by bridging the personal, cultural, and technical to preserve meaningful content for years to come.

This was a capstone project created for Professor Maher Elshakankiri’s class INF453: Capstone Project at the University of Toronto - St. George. Access to the mentioned resources and more in-depth descriptions can be retrieved from the project's official website preservr.me.

2. Research

To get a better understanding of the current landscape of social media in relation to digital archiving, our group identified 5 key subject areas to explore in a literature review:

  1. Digital archiving standards
  2. Social media platforms and their functionality
  3. Ethical and legal implications
  4. Existing tools
  5. Social media as cultural heritage
Social Media Platforms and Functionality Insights - Sehar
Archival Standards - Francesca
Existing Tools and Limitations - Luca
Ethics, Privacy, and Law Implications - Shirley
Case studies on personal digital archiving and the existing tools were also analyzed to gain a greater understanding of the current landscape. Beyond examining the landscape of the current tensions that exist in the areas that Preservr is founded upon, this literature review aimed to contextualize that information into actionable insights and factors that guide the ongoing development of Preservr as a tool and its ethos.

After completing our literature review, we identified 30 potential social media platforms that could be explored. The scope eventually was refined to 4 of arguably the most popular platforms: Instagram, Facebook, X (formerly Twitter), and Tik Tok, in which each group member was assigned a platform to create respective archiving workflow documentations. After presenting our findings to one another and deliberate discussion, the group decided to solely focus on Instagram to execute our project’s proof of concept.
