1. Overview

While past generations may have used physical photo albums and diaries to leave their legacies, our digital-native generation uses platforms like Instagram, TikTok, and Facebook to illustrate and tell their personal stories. Users lack reliable ways to safeguard their digital identities and narratives in the face of platform obsolescence, privacy risks, and shifting technological landscapes.

Preservr explored how an integrated tool suite can empower individuals to preserve their digital footprints while addressing challenges of organization, accessibility, sustainability, and heritage. The integrated tool suite allows people to curate their digital footprints by bridging the personal, cultural, and technical to preserve meaningful content for years to come.

This was a capstone project created for Professor Maher Elshakankiri’s class INF453: Capstone Project at the University of Toronto - St. George.

2. Research

Our project is grounded in three core ethical concerns: the erosion of user privacy through constant environmental surveillance (“soft biometrics”), the amplification of societal biases in training data, and the dangers of algorithmic opacity leading to digital colonialism (McStay 2020, Buolamwini & Gebru, 2018; Couldry & Mejias, 2019). By situating SDI within broader critiques of IoT and neoliberal data practices, we show how such technologies risk reinforcing structural inequalities under the guise of enhancing human connection.
